Jazz Resources & Media

Miles: The Autobiography

Very raw, unapologetic, and lots of fun, Miles’ Autobiography had me laughing out loud within the first few pages (drawing curious glances from my fellow passengers when I was reading it on the bus). Aside from Miles’ interesting opinions on jazz music (both his own and that of others), Miles has lots of highly personal anecdotes about his time spent with Dizzy Gillespie, Charlie Parker and other musicians.

Jazz Subreddit

The Jazz Subreddit is a great place to find newer material and more recommendations from various decades.

Jazz: A Film by Ken Burns

Running at over 1000 minutes, this 10-part documentary series contains tons of information and interesting stories. Although it’s treatment of post-50s material is somewhat unbalanced, it does a great job covering the beginnings of jazz up through the start of bebop. It’s a bit of a time investment, but I definitely think it’s worth it.
